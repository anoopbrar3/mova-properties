Design Vision · The Goldstream

01

A Form That Belongs

The Goldstream's massing responds to the natural topography of Langford — stepping with the hillside, not against it. The warm cream base and timber-panelled fins give the building material warmth uncommon in purpose-built rental. It looks like somewhere you'd choose, not settle for.

02

The Rooftop as Community

The crowning feature is a rooftop terrace that frames the forest and mountains beyond. In a market where amenity is an afterthought, this is a deliberate investment — a shared space that makes the building worth choosing and worth staying in, year after year.

03

Built for the Hold

CMHC MLI Select financing makes the Goldstream viable as a long-term hold — not a flip. Every material decision, every amenity choice, every layout is made with a 25-year owner's perspective. The result is a building that gets better with time, not older.

Project Overview

78 homes. A rooftop. A long-term address in a city still becoming itself.

Langford is one of the fastest-growing municipalities in British Columbia. The Goldstream positions MOVA at the leading edge of that growth — not by chasing it, but by building ahead of where the neighbourhood is going. At 77 suites across six storeys, this is MOVA's largest planned project to date.

Underground parking on two levels, a rooftop amenity deck, and suites designed with the same attention to proportion and material as MOVA's luxury single-family work. The Goldstream is proof that multifamily doesn't have to mean compromised.
