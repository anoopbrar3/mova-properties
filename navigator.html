The Project

A home built to hold its breath at the view — and exhale slowly through every room.

The Navigator sits near the summit of Bear Mountain, one of Greater Victoria's most coveted addresses. It was designed not to impose on its site, but to complete it — framing the ocean and valley views through floor-to-ceiling glazing that makes the Pacific Northwest feel like a painting you live inside of.


The great room is the heart of the project: a double-height volume anchored by a full-height fluted walnut feature wall and a book-matched dark marble fireplace that rises seventeen feet. A rain of crystal pendants descends from the ceiling. The room earns its name.

Every material decision in The Navigator was made in conversation with the light. The charcoal cabinetry absorbs daylight and glows warm at night. The walnut panels read differently at noon than at dusk. The brushed nickel and satin brass hardware was specified to work with both — never clashing, always cohesive.


This is what MOVA means when we say design is a financial discipline. The Navigator sold above asking. It sold quickly. It sold to buyers who understood that the difference between a good house and an exceptional one lives in the quality of the decisions made before anyone ever walks through the door.

Great Room

Double-Height Living

The defining space of The Navigator. Seventeen-foot ceiling volume anchored by a full-height fluted walnut feature wall and book-matched dark marble fireplace. A cascade of crystal pendant lights descends from above. Opens to a covered deck and uninterrupted ocean and valley views through floor-to-ceiling glazing.

Fluted walnut vertical stave, floor to ceiling

Book-matched dark marble fireplace surround

Crystal raindrop pendant chandelier — custom

LED strip at fireplace base and ceiling cove

Kitchen

The Working Heart

Charcoal flat-panel cabinetry surrounds a walnut island with a dark quartz waterfall counter. Amber blown-glass pendant lights hang low over bar seating. The stone backsplash runs from counter to ceiling — the same dark veined porcelain as the fireplace wall, creating a visual thread between rooms.

Charcoal matte cabinetry with satin brass 160mm pulls

Walnut island — waterfall dark quartz counter

Full-height stone backsplash — dark veined porcelain

Amber glass pendants · Pot filler in satin brass

Display cabinet — brass frame, interior illuminated

Powder Room

The Detail Room

The room guests remember. A full-round backlit mirror floats against geometric gold-on-taupe embossed wallpaper. An architectural brass pendant with globe lights hangs from the niche ceiling. Dark quartz vanity. Satin brass fixtures. The room is small — and unforgettable.

Custom geometric gold embossed wallpaper

Full-round LED backlit mirror — brass trim

Articulated brass globe pendant — 3-light orbital

Dark quartz vanity · Undermount sink · Brass tapware

Primary Ensuite

Light on Water

A freestanding soaker tub sits against full-width glazing framing treetops, sky, and the distant Strait of Georgia. A sculptural LED ring pendant hovers above. Steam shower with large-format greige tile, brushed nickel rain head, and frameless glass. Quiet, warm, and precise.

Freestanding soaker tub — ocean view aspect

Sculptural LED orbital pendant — matte black frame

Large-format greige tile steam shower · Rain head

Brushed nickel hardware · Floating walnut shelving

Lower Level

The Media Den

A full lower-level family and media room with a feature wall of vertical fluted natural oak stave framing a backlit large-format black stone tile surround. Floating charcoal media cabinet. The oak grain is warm and honey-toned — a lighter counterpoint to the upper-level walnut.

Vertical fluted natural oak stave feature wall

Backlit black crackle-glaze stone tile surround

Floating charcoal media console with drawers

In-ceiling speakers · LED strip cove lighting
