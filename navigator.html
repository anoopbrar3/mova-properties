Project Vision

A home that earns its place on the mountain.

The Navigator began with a single question: what does a Bear Mountain residence look like when every material decision is made without compromise? The answer took shape as a double-height great room anchored by a full-height marble fireplace, a fluted walnut feature wall, and unobstructed views of the ocean and valley below.

Every room was designed around how a family actually lives — generous proportions, considered flow, and a material palette that rewards time. Brass hardware. Large-format stone. Warm oak throughout. Nothing superfluous, nothing missing.

Interior Language

A calm, enduring palette that balances warmth and durability.

The Navigator's interior palette was designed to age with the home — materials that develop character rather than show wear. White oak ash millwork runs through the cabinetry and built-ins. American walnut provides richness at the stair and feature wall. Brushed brass punctuates without overpowering.

The Navigator is not a one-off. It is the most complete expression of the MOVA design standard — the same material thinking, spatial logic, and finish level we bring to every unit in our multifamily portfolio. When investors ask what they're buying, we show them this.
