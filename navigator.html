Design Vision · Navigators Rise

01

Drama by Architecture

The double-height great room is the home's centrepiece — not because it was added, but because the plan was built around it. Floor-to-ceiling glazing, a fireplace that reads from 20 feet away, and a staircase that becomes sculpture. Drama here is structural, not decorative.

02

The Kitchen as Statement

Grey-veined marble slab backsplash running floor to ceiling. Dark walnut island. Brass hardware. The kitchen at Navigators Rise doesn't retreat into functionality — it asserts itself as the room that defines the home. Every surface choice was made to be noticed.

03

Material Hierarchy

Every room in the home has a material that leads and materials that follow. Stone leads in the kitchen. Walnut leads in the office. White oak leads in the primary suite. The result is a home of distinct rooms, each with its own character — connected by a single, consistent point of view.
